Classic lasagna

This homemade lasagna is an Italian classic turned American favorite that's sure to be an instant addition to your recipe book. With a made-from-scratch meat sauce that's spruced up with McCormick® Herbs & Spices, our hearty lasagna recipe is packed full of rich, robust flavor. As with most lasagna dishes, this one includes a trio of cheeses — ricotta, mozzarella and Parmesan — for gooey goodness that'll have you coming back for more. Plus, no-boil noodles allow for easier preparation that's ideal for busy weeknights or when cooking a feast. To make it a complete meal, serve this meat lasagna with crusty Garlic Bread and a simple side salad of mixed greens, tomatoes and cucumbers.

Ingredients

Foe the meat sauce

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 small yellow onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 24 oz tomato sauce or marinara
  • 15 oz diced tomatoes (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper

For the Cheese Filling:

  • 15 oz Ricootta Cheese
  • 1/2 cup parmesan chees, grated
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese, shredded

Oder ingredients:

  • 9-12 lasagna noodles
  • 2 cups mozzarella cheese, shredded (for topping)
  • 1/4 cup parmesan cheese, grated( for topping)

Method

  1. preheat oven to 375°F(190°C).
  2. Cook ground beef with diced onion and garlic in alarge skillet over meduium heat until browne. Drain excess grease
  3. Add tomato sauce, diced tomates, basil, oregano, salt, and pepper. simmer for 10 ,imutes.
  4. In a bowl, combine ricotta, parmesan, beaten egg,and 2 cups mozzarella.
  5. Spread 1/2 cup of meat sauce in a 9x13 casserole dish. layer noodles, ricotta mixture, mozzarella, and meat sauce. repeat.
  6. Top with mozzarlle and parmesan.
  7. cover with foil and bake 25 minutes. remote foil and bake another 10 minute util bubbly.
  8. let it rest for 15 minute before serving.

Servings

Serves 6-8 people

Tips

  • For a vegetarian twist, omit ground beef and add zucchini, peppers, or spinach.
  • for substitions, you can use cottage chees instead of ricotta or gound turkey instead of beef.
